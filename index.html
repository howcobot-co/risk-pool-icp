Buying Behavior

1. Education & Problem Validation (1-3 months)

Research mode. Attend conferences, consume whitepapers, talk to peers. Want to understand scope of problem before evaluating solutions.

AWARENESS

2. Solution Exploration (2-4 months)

Evaluate 2-3 vendors. Prioritize proof of concept, peer validation, and ROI clarity. Want to see it working at similar pools.

CONSIDERATION

3. Pilot Design (1-2 months)

Negotiate pilot terms. Select 1-2 high-need member counties. Define success metrics. Get board approval for pilot budget.

EVALUATION

4. Pilot Execution (90-120 days)

Deploy at pilot sites. Track metrics religiously. Document outcomes and member feedback. Report to board and claims committee.

PILOT

5. Pool-Wide Adoption (Ongoing)

Board approves program recommendation. Pool endorses and funds/incentivizes. Members adopt through grants, credits, or preferred vendor status.

CUSTOMER

‚ö†Ô∏è Anti-Patterns (What NOT To Do)

  • Don't sell features. Sell outcomes. "30+ lives saved" > "24/7 monitoring with AI alerts"
  • Don't skip the pilot. Even warm leads want proof in their environment before scaling
  • Don't ignore risk control. Claims may have budget, but risk control owns adoption
  • Don't pitch only to healthcare. Pools want direct visibility, not contractor intermediation
  • Don't rush the board. They meet quarterly. Build pipeline around their schedule.

Pilot Structure (Critical to Success)

‚úì Pilot Best Practices

Duration: 90-120 days (long enough to see outcomes, short enough to maintain urgency)
Scope: 1-2 high-need member counties (not entire pool - too expensive/complex)
Success metrics: Lives saved, alert response times, documentation quality, staff feedback
Reporting: Monthly updates to pool, final presentation to board/committee
Funding: Pool-funded (ideal), member-funded with pool observation, or cost-share
Scale path: Pre-negotiated terms for pool-wide adoption if pilot succeeds
